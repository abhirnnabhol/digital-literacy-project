Part 1: Case Study – Phishing Attacks in the Tech Industry
Introduction:
Phishing is one of the most common and damaging cybercrimes today. It involves attackers masquerading as a trusted entity (like Google, Microsoft, or a University administration) to deceive individuals into disclosing sensitive information such as login credentials, credit card numbers, or personal identification.

The Scenario:
Imagine a first-year CSE student at VIT Bhopal receives an urgent email that appears to be from the "Google University Relations Team." The email congratulates the student on being shortlisted for a summer internship but states they must "log in to the student portal" within 24 hours to confirm their spot. The link in the email leads to a website that looks identical to the official Google login page.

The Impact:
If the student enters their Gmail credentials, the attacker gains full access to their account. For a student, this means the attacker can:

Access and delete their GitHub repositories and LeetCode progress.

Send fraudulent emails to professors or peers using the student's identity.

Access sensitive academic and financial records stored in the student's drive.

The Solution:
To prevent this, the student should have checked the sender's email address (often a slight misspelling like support@googl.com) and hovered over the link to see the actual URL. Most importantly, having Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) enabled would have blocked the attacker even after they stole the password.
